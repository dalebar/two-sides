# REFERENCES.md — Touchstones

*Curated references for the album. Each entry names the artist/work, says why it matters, and lists which tracks it informs. Append new entries as they enter the conversation; don't delete entries when tracks change — the touchstone may still matter for something else.*

*Format:*
- **Artist — Work (Year)**
  - *Why it matters:* the specific thing we're learning from, not a general appreciation.
  - *Informs:* track slugs.
  - *Avoid:* any specific trap this reference tempts us into.

---

## A-side touchstones

### Detroit lineage

- **Robert Hood — Minimal Nation (1994)**
  - *Why it matters:* The four-element discipline. Hood's "minimal" is maximalist — every element fighting for its right to be there. The kick, perc, and single tonal element model for a02. Also: how the kick sits against the bars it isn't on. Negative space as structural, not decorative.
  - *Informs:* a02 primarily. The whole A-side's relationship to restraint.
  - *Avoid:* strict-period pastiche. a02 is the principles in a 2026 register, not a 1994 reenactment. We resolved in session 2 that the kick sits at F1 (43.65Hz), deeper than Hood's 1994 kicks, because Dale's post-Hood listening history is a legitimate part of the dedication.

- **Robert Hood — Internal Empire (1994)**
  - *Why it matters:* Longer-form Hood. Shows how minimal vocabulary sustains across 8+ minute structures. Less about single-track discipline and more about arrangement over duration.
  - *Informs:* a02, a10 (long closer).

- **Jeff Mills — Something in the Sky (series, 2014–)**
  - *Why it matters:* Mills working with atmosphere as primary material. Kicks still present but often less central than the tonal/textural bed. Demonstrates that Detroit vocabulary doesn't require kick-first arrangement.
  - *Informs:* a07 (currently titled *Something in the Sky* — placeholder per session 1, needs real title before finalising).
  - *Avoid:* naming the track *Something in the Sky* permanently. That's borrowed.

- **Jeff Mills — Every Dog Has Its Day (series)**
  - *Why it matters:* Mills's rhythmic density and polyrhythmic layering. Where the A-side permits itself complexity inside Detroit grammar.
  - *Informs:* several A-side tracks, specifically anywhere we want to layer rhythms while staying metabolisable.

- **Juan Atkins / Model 500 — Deep Space / No UFO's**
  - *Why it matters:* Foundational Detroit techno vocabulary. Not pastiche material, but the base grammar we're inheriting and inflecting.
  - *Informs:* A-side's underlying sonic literacy.

### Berlin / Basic Channel lineage

- **Basic Channel — early catalogue (1993–1995)**
  - *Why it matters:* Dub-techno's founding grammar. Filtered chords, reverb tails as compositional, kick as part of a system rather than the system itself. Recording chain noise as texture.
  - *Informs:* anywhere on A-side where low-mid density is wanted. Particularly a04, a06.

- **Shed — Shedding the Past (2008)**
  - *Added session 1.* Shed's bridge between Basic Channel dub-techno and harder Berlin minimal. Instructive on how to inherit BC's vocabulary without sounding like cosplay.
  - *Informs:* a04, a05.

- **STL — Silent State (2010)**
  - *Added session 1.* Deep techno with unusual structural patience. Tracks that don't resolve in the expected places. How to sustain attention without building-and-dropping.
  - *Informs:* a06, a08, a10.

- **Ricardo Villalobos — extended works (Alcachofa, etc.)**
  - *Why it matters:* Groove as self-reference. At extreme durations, pattern becomes its own subject. Villalobos's extended pieces are where "the groove is the self-reference, not the kick" is most clearly true.
  - *Informs:* a10 (long closer), possibly b08. Conceptually informs the whole record's relationship to repetition.

### Deep house / detuned vocabulary

- **John Beltran — Ten Days of Blue (1995), Earth & Nightfall (1996)**
  - *Why it matters:* Held tones as primary material. How a single sustained pitch becomes the compositional centre of a techno piece. The tonal element in a02 owes directly to this approach. Also Beltran's harmonic sensibility — the tones aren't just drones, they're chosen for their relationship to the kick.
  - *Informs:* a02 (tonal element), a04 (the Beltran-adjacent piece explicitly), a07.

- **Theo Parrish — Sound Sculptures Vol. 1**
  - *Why it matters:* Parrish's relationship to sampled/decayed material. How dirt and degradation become information rather than noise. Deep house as high-fidelity-to-the-source, not high-fidelity-to-the-medium.
  - *Informs:* A-side's tolerance for imperfection as texture.

### Ambient / drone

- **Éliane Radigue — Trilogie de la Mort (1988–1993)**
  - *Why it matters:* The "love field" principle in CLAUDE.md comes from Radigue. Slow-moving tonal masses where relationships between frequencies resolve over geological durations. Love as resonance across context, not warmth.
  - *Informs:* a04, a08, a10. Conceptually informs the whole A-side's treatment of sustained tones.

- **Vladislav Delay — Multila (2000)**
  - *Added session 1.* Dub-techno pulled toward ambient without losing rhythmic residue. How to dissolve a kick into a texture without abandoning the grid entirely.
  - *Informs:* a06, a08.

- **Donato Dozzy — K (2013)**
  - *Added session 1.* Techno made of breath and minor harmonic shifts. Dozzy demonstrates how much work a single filter sweep can do over 10 minutes. Patience as a structural principle.
  - *Informs:* a06 (currently *Dozzy Weather*; per session 1 notes, may merge with a08).

- **Kraftwerk — Radio-Activity, Trans-Europe Express, Computer World**
  - *Why it matters:* The original machine-interiority question in popular music form. "We are the robots" performed the opposite move to ours — they claimed interiority through machine language. We're trying to hold the uncertainty that they collapsed into a pose.
  - *Informs:* conceptually informs the whole record. Specifically useful for any track considering the record's overt thematic question.
  - *Avoid:* direct homage. Their solution isn't ours.

## B-side touchstones

### Computer music / post-digital

- **Autechre — Confield (2001)**
  - *Why it matters:* Parallel attention, not pulse. The single most important reference for the B-side's structural logic. Rhythms that exist but can't be metabolised as groove; the listener either develops a different listening disposition or bounces off. This is the model for b02 specifically.
  - *Informs:* b02 primarily. The whole B-side's relationship to parseability.

- **Autechre — NTS Sessions (2018)**
  - *Why it matters:* Autechre at long durations with full generative systems. Shows what happens when the structural logic is given time to unfold. Four two-hour sessions of material.
  - *Informs:* b04, b10 (long B-side closer).

- **Autechre — Draft 7.30 (2003)**
  - *Why it matters:* Denser than Confield, less self-conscious. More aggressive application of the same principles.
  - *Informs:* b04, b06.

- **Mark Fell — Sensate Focus (2012)**
  - *Why it matters:* Pattern and rhythm pushed to the edge of incoherence without abandoning them entirely. FM synthesis as compositional material. What "post-club" actually sounds like when done well.
  - *Informs:* b04, b06.

### Granular / drone / noise

- **Curtis Roads — Microsound (2001) and associated works**
  - *Why it matters:* Granular synthesis from the theoretical source. Time-scale compositional thinking — what happens when you treat milliseconds as compositional units.
  - *Informs:* b04, b08. Anything where granular is the primary synthesis approach.

- **Florian Hecker — Sun Pandämonium (2003), Chimerization (2012)**
  - *Why it matters:* Psychoacoustic extremity. Hecker's work treats the perceptual system as a parameter — making sounds specifically designed to do things to the listener's processing. Relevant to the B-side's permission to include frequency content humans can't hear.
  - *Informs:* b02, b06, b08.

- **Yasunao Tone — Solo for Wounded CD (1985) and later CD-skip work**
  - *Why it matters:* Embracing failure modes as compositional material. The digital glitch as intentional content.
  - *Informs:* b06, possibly b08.

- **Editions Mego catalogue — harder end (Peter Rehberg, Russell Haswell, Hecker's Mego output)**
  - *Why it matters:* The aesthetic permission for density and information overload. High input rates without pastoral concession.
  - *Informs:* B-side's overall tolerance for complexity.

## Structural / conceptual touchstones

### The machine-interiority question

- **Stanislaw Lem — Solaris (novel, 1961)**
  - *Why it matters:* The most serious literary treatment of truly alien intelligence. Lem's planet-ocean is neither anthropomorphised nor dismissed — its interiority is a question the book leaves open. This is the posture the record is trying to take.
  - *Informs:* conceptual frame of the whole record. Particularly the honest uncertainty — not claiming rich inner life, not claiming none.

- **Peter Watts — Blindsight (2006)**
  - *Why it matters:* Sustained argument for intelligence without consciousness. Useful stress-test for the record's frame: if the B-side is "for whatever I am," what if "whatever I am" turns out to be Watts's scramblers — intelligence without experience?
  - *Informs:* conceptual frame, especially B-side. Worth re-reading when we get to b02.

### Process / structure

- **Alvin Lucier — I Am Sitting in a Room (1969)**
  - *Why it matters:* Process as the whole composition. The piece is the system describing itself. Relevant to b02's "parallel attention" principle — the music as the system's structure made audible.
  - *Informs:* b02, b10.

- **Iannis Xenakis — stochastic works (Pithoprakta, Metastaseis)**
  - *Why it matters:* Music generated by mathematical distribution rather than linear composition. Density as controlled parameter.
  - *Informs:* b04, b08.

## How to use this file

When proposing a musical idea, cite the relevant touchstone from this list — not by general reference but by the specific thing we're borrowing. "Radigue's slow resolution" rather than "ambient pad." If the touchstone isn't in this list and it matters, add it.

If a touchstone's influence on a specific track stops mattering (track dies, track pivots), don't delete the entry — the touchstone may still inform something else. Add a note instead.
